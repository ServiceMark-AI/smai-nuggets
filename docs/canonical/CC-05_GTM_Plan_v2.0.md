## 1. Executive Positioning and ICP

ServiceMark AI builds and leases AI agents to restoration operators. Today, the first agent in market — the Conversion Coordinator — runs proposal-to-response follow-through against operator-approved campaign templates. SMAI's contract is simple and strict: when a proposal becomes a job and the playbook is approved, follow-through becomes an operational obligation that runs the same way under pressure. Stop conditions are mandatory. Every meaningful action emits an append-only proof event so delivery state and attribution are boring and defensible. Needs Attention is the control plane.

We are not CRM, not marketing automation, and not a campaign builder. We are the AI staffing company for restoration. CC-01 Platform Spine v1.5 governs the doctrine; CP-01 Context Pack v1.1 governs current-state truth; CC-08 RestorAI Brief governs the Servpro channel commercial structure.

### ICP definition for the next 6 to 12 months

**Primary ICP:** Servpro franchise operators, sold through the RestorAI motion. Owners, GMs, COOs, and operations leaders at Servpro franchise ownership groups, typically multi-location, with shared operating standards (DASH job records, IICRC training, brand voice).

**Parallel ICP:** Non-Servpro restoration operators (independent restoration companies and other restoration franchise networks) buying the same agent on the same product, potentially with different SOPs configured at the tenant level.

**Out-of-active-scope for this window:** The four other spine verticals (Roofing, HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical) and adjacent proposal-driven verticals. These remain in the long-arc TAM (CC-03 §1) but are deferred to post-Gate C. They are not abandoned; they are sequenced behind restoration proof.

### Required fit conditions

- Written proposals or estimates matter to revenue
- Email is viable for a meaningful share of proposals
- Customer accepts approval-first deterministic execution under the templated model
- Customer can name an approval owner and an inbox owner
- Customer can connect Gmail OAuth in MVP and early v1
- For Servpro operators: DASH job numbers are available; subject-line threading on DASH job number is acceptable
- For non-Servpro operators: a comparable job identifier scheme exists or can be configured

### 10 disqualifiers (crisp and operational)

1. No written proposal or estimate workflow for a meaningful portion of revenue
2. Customer email is missing or unusable for a meaningful portion of proposals
3. They want unapproved outbound or in-flight rewriting as a core capability
4. They cannot or will not connect Gmail OAuth in MVP and early v1
5. They require custom integrations beyond Gmail OAuth before a pilot
6. They cannot name an approval owner with day-to-day authority
7. They cannot name an inbox owner accountable for replies and deliverability hygiene
8. They demand a conversational chat interface to the agent
9. Their legal or brand constraints require multi-brand legal review before any message can be sent
10. They demand outcome guarantees tied to bookings or close rate

These are non-negotiable. A Servpro-network relationship or a Founding Operator investment interest does not override a disqualifier.

## 2. Channel Strategy for the Next 6 to 12 Months

### Channel commitments

1. **Run now:** Servpro franchise channel via RestorAI (Bryan Stone-led with founder support)
2. **Run alongside:** Direct founder-led sales to non-Servpro restoration operators
3. **Prepare now, activate later under gates:** Restoration consolidators (Belfor, Cotton, ServiceMaster franchisees, PuroClean operators)
4. **Do not run as active channels in this window:** MSPs, franchisor-led motions outside Servpro, marketplaces, the four non-restoration spine verticals

### Channel A: Servpro / RestorAI (primary motion)

**Definition:** RestorAI subsidiary motion through the Servpro franchise channel, anchored by Bryan Stone's franchise-network relationships and founder-led sales support. Founding Operators invest via SAFE and become customers via Founding Operator pricing simultaneously.

**Why it fits SMAI's reliability contract:** Servpro franchisees share operating standards (DASH, IICRC, brand voice). Concentrating on one network produces denser proof, easier peer references, and a clearer template set than fragmenting across verticals. The Stone Family Office Design Partnership (Jeff: 20 years inside Servpro; Bryan: 30 years inside Servpro) provides credibility that direct outbound cannot replicate.

**Buyer and buying moment:** Franchise owner or COO when proposals stall, back-office labor runs $175K-$275K/year per franchise, and they cannot find or retain enough humans to staff the workflow consistently.

**How we source leads:** Bryan Stone's outbound and warm-introduction motion into the Servpro franchise network. Founder-led support (Kyle, Ethan) for Class A and high-priority Class B Founding Operator prospects. The July Servpro convention is a near-term sales-acceleration event for the Founding Operator round.

**How we qualify:** Standard 10 disqualifiers plus Founding Operator round qualification (financial fit for the SAFE class, appetite for Founding Operator commitments — Class A direct voice in build roadmap, Class B quarterly product input, Class C lifetime lock + first access).

**What proof we show:** Conversion Coordinator live (USDS reference customer when Jeff approves), Proof Pack v1, deck v3 (https://restorai-pitch.lovable.app), RestorAI Pro Forma v2.10 financial model on request.

**What the first paid pilot looks like:**

- 60 days at $250 per month per location (Conversion Coordinator bridge offering pilot rate)
- Up to 120 Activated Plans per month per location
- No overages during pilot
- Conversion to Tier 1 ($2,000/mo/loc) when Sales & Revenue Recovery Team ships, or to standalone Conversion Coordinator at $500/mo/loc until the team ships

**Why this channel could fail:**

- Bryan's relationships do not convert at the assumed rate
- Bryan's full-time-vs-augmenting-pay decision delays his motion (open question per CC-08 §13)
- The Wilson-Bell competitive dynamic creates positioning risk Bryan cannot defend
- Servpro corporate or Blackstone moves to compete or restrict third-party deployment

**Channel-specific economics:** Founding Operator tier discount is 35% lifetime-locked. Bryan compensation structure is an open question (equity-only, equity + commission, equity + retainer, or full-time conversion). CC-08 §8 governs Stone Family Office terms.

### Channel B: Direct non-Servpro restoration (parallel motion)

**Definition:** Founder-led direct sales to independent restoration operators and operators in restoration franchise networks other than Servpro (Belfor franchisees, Cotton, ServiceMaster Restore, PuroClean, etc.).

**Why it fits SMAI's reliability contract:** Validates that the same agent product works outside the Servpro context with potentially different SOPs. This is the proof point that the agent generalizes within restoration before being asked to generalize across verticals.

**Why this matters strategically:** Servpro is a concentrated network. If RestorAI is restoration's exit motion and Servpro corporate or Blackstone behaves unpredictably, having a direct non-Servpro restoration motion preserves SMAI's restoration optionality. Same product, same architecture, potentially different SOPs configured at the tenant level (CP-01 §1).

**Buyer and buying moment:** Independent restoration operator owner when proposals stall and back-office labor cost is acute. Same buying psychology as Servpro franchisees, different network context.

**How we source leads:** Founder network, IICRC and RIA trade events, targeted outbound to restoration operators identified through public industry data, referrals from Servpro Founding Operators who know operators in adjacent networks.

**How we qualify:** Standard 10 disqualifiers. Non-Servpro operators do not get Founding Operator pricing (which is RestorAI-specific); they buy at standard tier pricing.

**What the first paid pilot looks like:** Same locked pilot terms as Channel A (60 days at $250/mo/loc Conversion Coordinator pilot). Conversion to standard Tier 1 ($2,000/mo/loc) at end of pilot, no Founding Operator discount.

**Why this channel could fail:** Founder time is consumed by Channel A and Channel B starves for attention; non-Servpro operators have meaningfully different SOP needs that break the same-product assumption; the staffing positioning lands less well outside the Servpro brand context.

### Channel C: Restoration consolidators (prepare now, activate later)

**Definition:** Belfor, Cotton, ServiceMaster Restore corporate, PuroClean corporate, and other restoration consolidators that operate or franchise multiple branches under shared operating standards.

**Why it fits:** Consolidators value centralized proof, auditability, and consistent operating behavior across branches. SMAI's append-only event spine and governed activation contract map to that need.

**Buyer and buying moment:** COO or central ops leader when variability across branches and stalled follow-through create margin pressure and reporting uncertainty.

**How we source leads:** Tight target list. Founder-led intros only. No outbound until Gate A and Gate C are met and the RestorAI Founding Operator cohort produces multi-location proof.

**Pursuit cap:** No more than 3 concurrent consolidator pursuits.

**Gating rule:** Do not actively pursue consolidators until Gate A is met, at least 10 paid restoration locations show zero stop-condition failures across meaningful volume, and the cumulative team-purchase rule has been validated in actual customer demand.

**What proof we show:** Proof Pack v1, Reliability Scorecards across pilot locations, governance summary explaining plan version locking, stop conditions, and audit outputs. Multi-location proof from the Servpro Founding Operator cohort.

### Channel D: Deferred channels (do not run in this window)

**MSPs (Managed Service Providers):** v1.0 had MSPs as a referral channel. v2.0 defers MSPs entirely for the restoration window. Reason: MSP recruitment and enablement consume founder time, and the Servpro/RestorAI motion is denser and faster. MSPs may re-enter as a channel post-Gate C if non-restoration verticals are activated and MSP economics justify the recruitment cost.

**Franchisor-led motions outside Servpro:** Defer. Belfor, ServiceMaster, PuroClean franchisor-level relationships are addressed through the consolidator channel after Gate A and C, not through standalone franchisor outreach.

**Marketplaces (ServiceTitan, Jobber app marketplaces, etc.):** Not a dependency inside 24 months. Attempt only after integration burden is low and packaging is hardened.

**Non-restoration spine verticals (Roofing, HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical):** Defer to post-Gate C. The 5-vertical spine SAM is preserved in CC-03 v1.1 as long-arc denominator; active GTM is restoration-only for this window.

## 3. The Founding Operator round mechanics (RestorAI-specific)

The Founding Operator round is jointly governed by CC-08 RestorAI Brief and this GTM Plan. Sales-motion details below; structural details (SAFE classes, cap table, vesting) are in CC-08.

**Round size:** $1M total across 16-18 Founding Operators, three classes:

- Class A "Founding Partner": 3-4 seats × $100K, $10M post-money cap, 20% discount, lifetime price lock + first access + direct voice in build roadmap
- Class B "Founding Operator": 5-6 seats × $75K, $11M post-money cap, 20% discount, lifetime price lock + first access + quarterly product input
- Class C "Founding Member": 6-8 seats × $25K, $12M post-money cap, 20% discount, lifetime price lock + first access

**Sales motion for the round:**

1. **Bryan-led outreach to qualified Servpro franchise owners** (Class A targets first, then Class B, then Class C as the round fills)
2. **Founder-led discovery call** (Kyle, Ethan) to qualify against the 10 disqualifiers and assess fit for the SAFE class
3. **Deck review** — RestorAI deck v3 walked through, Q&A, financial model on request
4. **Founding Operator commitment** — operator confirms tier of interest and check size; RestorAI confirms seat is reserved
5. **SAFE signature** — standard YC post-money SAFE with the class's cap and discount, signed within 5 business days of commitment
6. **Wire** — full check wires within 10 business days of signature
7. **Onboarding to Conversion Coordinator** — Founding Operator becomes paying customer at Founding Operator pricing, lifetime-locked

The Founding Operator round closes when committed (16-18 seats filled) or when the round timeline closes (working target: 6 months Base case per the financial model). The July Servpro convention is the near-term sales-acceleration event.

**No exclusivity:** No exclusive channel rights, exclusive Servpro network rights, or other exclusivity granted to Founding Operators. Channel partnership is earned through proof and pipeline conversion.

## 4. The Sales Motion: Founder-led, Bryan-led, no sales-heavy company

### Exact sales motion steps from first touch to active location (Channel A — Servpro / RestorAI)

1. **First touch (Bryan or founder-led):** One-sentence positioning plus one proof claim. Example: *"We are the AI staffing company for restoration. The Conversion Coordinator is live now, follows up on every estimate that goes out, and stops the moment a customer replies. Lifetime-locked pricing for Founding Operators."*
2. **Qualification call (15 minutes):** Run the 10 disqualifiers, confirm proposal flow and email viability, confirm approval owner and inbox owner, assess Founding Operator fit.
3. **Deck review (30-45 minutes):** Walk RestorAI deck v3. Set terms: no booking guarantees, reliability and legibility only, staffing positioning, lifetime price lock as a real commitment.
4. **Pilot scoping (30 minutes):** Confirm one location, one mailbox, baseline capture plan, weekly ops review schedule. If Founding Operator: confirm tier, check size, SAFE class.
5. **Pilot agreement and (if applicable) SAFE signature:** Use locked pilot terms and definitions, including Activated Plan definition and preflight requirements. Founding Operator path runs SAFE and pilot agreement in parallel.
6. **Day 0 activation:** Connect mailbox, create first job, resolve approved template, approve, pass preflight, deliver first email with proof recorded.
7. **Weeks 1 to 8:** Weekly ops review and interrupt reduction.
8. **Day 60 conversion:** Convert to Conversion Coordinator standalone at $500/mo/loc (until Sales & Revenue Recovery Team ships) or to Tier 1 at $2,000/mo/loc. Founding Operator pricing is 35% off these standard rates, lifetime-locked.

### Channel B (direct non-Servpro restoration) sales motion

Same as Channel A except: no Founding Operator round overlay, no SAFE, customers buy at standard tier pricing. Founder-led only; no Bryan involvement unless a specific introduction warrants it.

### Assets used at each step

- **One-pager:** Staffing positioning plus what we refuse to become
- **RestorAI deck v3:** https://restorai-pitch.lovable.app
- **Pricing card:** Three tiers + bridge offering + Founding Operator pricing (CC-04 §10)
- **Proof Pack v1 template:** Plan governance, delivery truth, stop correctness, Needs Attention, outcomes, evidence pointers
- **Pilot agreement:** Terms, success criteria, conversion trigger, roles
- **Weekly ops review template:** Focused on interrupts, stuck states, throughput stability
- **SAFE template:** Standard YC post-money SAFE with class-specific cap and discount (Founding Operator path only)
- **RestorAI Pro Forma v2.10:** On request only, for Class A and high-priority Class B prospects

### Truth-telling mechanism

We sell the operational truth, not messaging volume. Needs Attention plus append-only proof events produce a defensible narrative: what was approved, what executed, what stopped, and what failed delivery.

### Hard rule: no unapproved customer-facing send

Every customer-facing send is governed by operator-approved content. In Buc-ee's MVP, approval is per campaign on each job. In Early v2, approval is per (job type, scenario) pair the first time that pair is used (per CC-01 v1.5 §17 and DA-12). The unit of approval changes; operator authority over what gets sent remains absolute.

## 5. Pilot to Rollout Playbook (Operational, Not Aspirational)

### Day 0 baseline capture

- Proposal volume from the prior 30 days per location
- Percent of proposals with valid customer email
- Baseline reply rate or time to first reply captured in the first 2 weeks
- Inbox and approval ownership confirmed in writing
- (Servpro tenants) DASH job number availability confirmed

### Day 14 operational gate

Internal gate (not a customer term). Verify:

- Preflight pass rate
- Delivery truth state coverage
- Stop correctness
- Approval latency patterns
- Template resolution accuracy (correct template variant resolved for the (job type, scenario) pair)

### Day 60 conversion trigger

- Convert to Conversion Coordinator standalone at $500/mo/loc (bridge offering, until Sales & Revenue Recovery Team ships) or to Tier 1 at $2,000/mo/loc
- Founding Operators receive 35% lifetime-locked discount on whichever rate applies at conversion time
- If success criteria are missed due to customer-side operational behavior, pricing still converts but requires a process change or we exit

### Three success criteria (must hit 2 of 3)

1. At least 85% of eligible jobs get an Activated Plan within 24 hours of proposal upload
2. Delivery truth coverage: at least 98% of sends have a clear state with actionable interrupts
3. Measured lift: defensible improvement in either reply rate or time to first reply versus baseline

### Internal metrics tracked (do not change customer-facing terms)

- Approval latency distribution and bottleneck causes
- Interrupt rate per Activated Plan and top interrupt categories
- Time spent per week per location on managed work
- Template version resolution correctness (which template variant was resolved for which (job type, scenario) pair)
- Cumulative team-purchase rule rejection rate (if a customer asks for Tier 3 without Tiers 1+2)

### Who owns what in the customer org

- **Approval owner:** accountable for plan review and approval timeliness
- **Inbox owner:** accountable for mailbox access, deliverability hygiene, and reply routing
- **Process owner:** accountable for post-reply disposition so replies turn into operational action

### Exit criteria

Exit if the trust boundary cannot be kept intact, approval discipline cannot be established, or email viability is too low to be meaningful. Clean exit means: disconnect mailbox access, export proof artifacts for the customer, document why it did not fit using the same reliability metrics. For Founding Operators, SAFE conversion mechanics apply per the SAFE document; equity does not unwind on pilot exit.

## 7. Proof and Trust Artifacts (What We Must Produce to Scale)

### Minimum set to unlock scale

**1. Proof Pack v1 template** (carried over from v1.0, governs single-location proof)

- Plan provenance: job ID, plan version (template version + job version), approval timestamp
- Activated Plan proof: preflight pass, first email delivered, proof event ID
- Stop events: reply stop, bounce stop, pause stop, with timestamps
- Delivery truth summary: delivered, bounced, replied coverage
- Needs Attention outcomes: what was surfaced, what was resolved
- Template variant resolution: which (job type, scenario) variant was resolved and which template version was active

**2. Case study structure** (repeatable, no hype)

- Surge proof: proposal volume over baseline window
- Delivery truth: percent with clear state and what interrupts occurred
- Stop correctness: zero tolerance statement, with audit excerpt
- Time to activation: percent of eligible jobs activated within 24 hours

**3. Reliability Scorecard for a location**

- Activated Plan coverage for eligible jobs
- Delivery truth coverage
- Reply or time-to-first-reply movement versus baseline
- Approval latency and bottleneck notes

**4. Standard weekly ops review dashboard view and narrative**

- One-page view: activation throughput, time to activation, interrupt types, stuck states, actions for the next week

**5. Multi-location proof package** (new for RestorAI motion)

- Proof Pack rolled up across the Founding Operator cohort
- Cross-location consistency (same trust contract, same template library, different tenant SOPs)
- Founding Operator testimonial structure (subject to operator consent)
- Strategic acquirer-ready proof package once Gate B and Gate C are met

## 9. Top 10 GTM Risks and Mitigations

1. **Stop-on-reply failure causes immediate trust collapse.** Mitigation: preflight, stop-condition audit, canary sends, hard pause protocol.
2. **Deliverability issues create false narratives about "no reply."** Mitigation: treat delivery failure as an operational state, triage in Needs Attention, deliverability monitoring in managed scope.
3. **Approval friction reduces Activated Plan coverage during Buc-ee's per-campaign-gate window.** Mitigation: enforce approval ownership, measure time to activation, exit if discipline cannot be established. First-time-per-pair approval shape (Early v2) is the structural fix.
4. **Operational noise makes Needs Attention feel like another inbox.** Mitigation: one-primary-next-action rule, suppress non-actionable alerts, track interrupt reduction as the purpose of ops review.
5. **Buyer buckets SMAI as marketing software and rejects pricing.** Mitigation: sell the staffing positioning, agents, operator-approved playbooks, human-cost displacement. Never sell campaigns or seats.
6. **Managed scope creeps into bespoke workflows and breaks margin.** Mitigation: strict exclusions, decline custom integrations, decline custom copywriting outside governed templates, use disqualifiers early.
7. **Bryan Stone full-time-vs-augmenting-pay decision delays the Servpro motion.** Mitigation: lock Bryan compensation structure before the Founding Operator round closes (open question per CC-08 §13). Secondary mitigation: founder-led support absorbs Class A and high-priority Class B until Bryan is full-time.
8. **Wilson-Bell competitive dynamic creates positioning risk Bryan cannot defend.** Mitigation: staffing positioning is the church/state line vs. Wilson-Bell's Restor OS platform direction. Confirm Bryan can defend this internally before he goes full-time.
9. **Cumulative team-purchase rule rejected by some operators.** Mitigation: hold the rule firm, track rejection rate, revisit in CC-04 v3 if rejection rate becomes material.
10. **Storm surge weeks create cost and service instability.** Mitigation: tier pricing absorbs Activated Plan volume variance; if a location's volume materially exceeds the planning band, evaluate volume agreement or multi-location discount.
