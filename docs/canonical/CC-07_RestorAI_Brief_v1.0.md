## 1. What RestorAI is

**RestorAI is the AI staffing company for restoration.** It is a wholly-controlled subsidiary of ServiceMark AI (SMAI) capitalized through Servpro franchise-owner SAFE investment, organized as the dedicated go-to-market entity for the Servpro franchise channel and adjacent restoration operators.

The positioning is staffing, not software:

> *"We are not selling you software. We are staffing your back office."*

That sentence is the deck's anchor and the canonical positioning for RestorAI. It governs every commercial conversation, every public-facing artifact, and every pricing page. Differentiation against Breezy, CompanyCam, Hatch, ServiceTitan, and Wilson-Bell's "Restor OS" platform direction is built on this line. RestorAI sells AI employees that follow operator-approved SOPs, not configurable software the customer must operate.

RestorAI's product is the SMAI agent fleet, deployed to restoration operators under the team-based pricing model defined in CP-01 §7. There is no separate RestorAI product, no separate codebase, no separate platform spine, no separate trust contract. Those all live in SMAI. RestorAI is the **commercial entity**, the **brand**, the **channel motion**, and the **capitalization vehicle** for restoration.

Operating identity:

- RestorAI is a Delaware C-corp structured as a subsidiary of SMAI with SMAI holding 80% pre-SAFE equity
- The Stone family (Jeff and Bryan) hold 5% each direct, with 4-year/1-year-cliff vesting from agreement signing
- A 10% RestorAI option pool is reserved for future RestorAI-specific employees and advisors
- RestorAI operates under an arm's-length IP license from SMAI for the restoration vertical and the Servpro franchise channel
- SMAI retains all platform IP and direct-sales rights to all other field service verticals (HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, and adjacent proposal-driven verticals)
- RestorAI is positioned for strategic acquisition in the next 12 to 36 months; SMAI's other verticals are not part of that exit

## 2. What RestorAI is not

These boundaries protect SMAI's platform IP, the trust contract, channel discipline, and the multi-vertical thesis that defines SMAI's longer arc.

- **Not a product fork.** RestorAI tenants run on the SMAI codebase. There is no RestorAI-specific code path, no RestorAI-specific surface, no RestorAI-specific trust contract.
- **Not a brand replacement for SMAI.** SMAI continues to exist as the platform parent and as the direct-sales vehicle for non-restoration verticals.
- **Not a license to violate the trust contract.** Every RestorAI tenant is governed by the same approval-first, deterministic execution, immediate stop, append-only proof discipline that governs SMAI direct tenants (CC-01 §17).
- **Not a bespoke services arm.** RestorAI does not commit to per-customer custom workflows, custom integrations beyond Gmail OAuth in MVP and early v1, or per-customer template authoring outside the SPEC-12 governed methodology.
- **Not a guarantee of Servpro corporate endorsement.** The relationship is with individual Servpro franchise owners, not with Servpro corporate or Blackstone. Corporate endorsement is a possible long-term outcome, not a near-term assumption.
- **Not a public per-role rate card.** Internal pricing is the per-role rate card (governed by RestorAI Pro Forma Roles & Bundles tab); customer-facing pricing is the three ratcheted tiers (CP-01 §7). À la carte single-role purchase is not a standard SKU.

## 3. Why RestorAI exists (the strategic logic)

**1. Capital efficiency through Founding Operator investment.** Servpro franchise owners are the operators who most clearly benefit from the agent fleet and the operators most able to validate it. Letting them invest equity at the subsidiary level aligns incentives, accelerates capitalization beyond what organic SaaS revenue would support, and gives them ROI through three mechanisms simultaneously: labor-displacement savings at their own locations, equity appreciation at acquisition, and lifetime-locked discount on their subscription tier (the Founding Operator price).

**2. Channel concentration and proof density.** Servpro is a 1,800-to-2,000-location U.S. franchise network with shared operating standards (DASH job records, IICRC training, brand voice). Concentrating GTM on one network produces denser proof, easier peer references, and a clearer template set than fragmenting effort across five spine verticals. Bryan Stone's franchise-network sales motion is the demand-generation mechanism; Jeff Stone's role as Founding Design Partner is the credibility anchor.

**3. Strategic optionality at exit.** Blackstone owns Servpro corporate and is approximately 6 years into a typical 5-to-8-year PE hold. They need to demonstrate franchisee value creation before exit. A RestorAI entity owned in part by legitimate Servpro franchise owners, deployed across a meaningful percentage of the network, and producing measurable cost displacement, becomes a credible acquisition target for Blackstone (defensive: prevent a competitor from buying it; offensive: bundle into Servpro corporate's value proposition). If Blackstone does not acquire, the entity is pivotable to Belfor, ServiceMaster, PuroClean, Cotton, or comparable restoration consolidators.

**4. Multi-vertical platform preservation.** RestorAI is the entity that gets sold in a strategic exit. SMAI is not. The structure preserves SMAI's optionality across HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, and adjacent verticals, while letting restoration earn an outsized exit on a compressed timeline. Founder economics (Kyle, Ethan, Mark) flow through SMAI's ownership of RestorAI, not through direct RestorAI grants — which means a RestorAI exit returns capital to founders without ending the SMAI platform.

## 4. Capitalization structure (Founding Operator round)

This is the locked structure per the v2.10 financial model. Terms subject to counsel review and final SAFE drafting; structural decisions are settled.

**Round size:** $1,000,000 total Founding Operator round, structured as SAFEs across three classes. **16-18 Founding Operators total** (deck Slide 17). First-come allocation.

**SAFE class structure** (per deck Slide 17, with per-class commitments):

| Class | Title | Seats | Check size | Cap | Discount | Per-class commitments |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Class A | Founding Partner | 3-4 | $100,000 | $10M | 20% | Lifetime price lock, first access to every new digital employee, **direct voice in build roadmap** |
| Class B | Founding Operator | 5-6 | $75,000 | $11M | 20% | Lifetime price lock, first access to every new digital employee, **quarterly product input** |
| Class C | Founding Member | 6-8 | $25,000 | $12M | 20% | Lifetime price lock, first access to every new digital employee |

**Class semantics:**

- **Class A (Founding Partner)** is reserved for the largest franchise ownership groups, most strategic introductions, or legacy Servpro family shareholders. Best terms (lowest cap), seat at the build-roadmap table.
- **Class B (Founding Operator)** is the standard tier for engaged franchise owners with multi-location operations. Quarterly product input cycle.
- **Class C (Founding Member)** is the entry tier for smaller franchise operators or operators who want skin in the game without a six-figure check.

**Conversion mechanics:** Post-money SAFEs converting at the next equity round (Series A) or at a liquidity event (acquisition), whichever comes first. Standard YC SAFE structure. Converts to preferred stock at Series A at the more favorable of valuation cap or 20% discount. Subject to legal finalization.

**Founding Operator subscription discount (separate from SAFE):** Every Founding Operator receives a **lifetime-locked discount of 35% off standard tier pricing** on whichever tier they purchase. This is the operating-side complement to the SAFE: investors are also customers, and they are buying both equity exposure and discounted subscription pricing. The 35% lock is base case (30% Conservative, 40% Aggressive in the financial model).

**5-year savings framing (deck Slide 17):**

- Class A at 5 locations: **~$135K saved + equity upside**
- Class B at 3 locations: **~$80K saved + equity upside**
- Class C at 1 location: **~$27K saved + equity upside**

(Calculated on Tier 1 Revenue Recovery at $24K/year per location with 35% Founding Operator discount, with 5% annual standard pricing escalation across 5 years.)

**Per-class commitments are real product/operational commitments**, not marketing language. Class A's "direct voice in build roadmap" and Class B's "quarterly product input" both create governance touchpoints that need operational design (cadence, format, scope of input vs. decision authority). Specifics to be defined before the first Class A SAFE closes.

**Year 1 cash-flow neutral framing (deck Slide 16):** Year 1 spending ~$740K, Year 1 revenue ~$759K. The $1M raise is the reserve that funds Year 2 hiring and the strategic acquisition window — not the Year 1 operating budget.

**$1M raise allocation (deck Slide 16):**

- Team & operations: $324K (32%) — 3 founders at $90K base + 20% benefits, below seed-stage market rate by 25-30%
- AI infrastructure & tokens: $267K (27%) — Claude Sonnet API, GCP infrastructure, telephony, transcription, voice synthesis

## 6. The exit window and optionality framing

The deck's frame is *"the strategic acquirer is inevitable; we choose if or when we sell."* Not a binary path choice. One continuous timeline with two visible exit points and Series A as a bridge if neither point produces a strategic offer the team wants to accept.

**ARR trajectory (Base to Aggressive):**

- Year 1 (Founding Round + Pilot Wave): 26-37 customers, ~65-95 locations, **$1.7M-$2.5M ARR**
- Year 2 (Post-Proof Expansion): 94-140 customers, ~235-350 locations, **$8M-$14M ARR**
- Year 3 (Category Position): 258-380 customers, ~645-950 locations, **$25M-$32M ARR**

**Two exit points within the window:**

**Acceleration scenario (~Year 1.5):**

- $5M ARR at exit
- Strategic exit at ~$175M (~35x ARR with strategic urgency premium)
- Strategic acquirer moves early; faster return, smaller absolute outcome
- SAFE MOIC: ~14x at this exit point

**Planning case (Year 3):**

- $25M-$32M ARR at exit
- Strategic exit at $760M-$960M (15x ARR with full 2x strategic premium)
- Mid-range case: $760M
- Strategic acquirer moves at category maturity; larger absolute outcome
- SAFE MOIC: ~45x at this exit point

**Series A as bridge, not as primary path.** If a strategic acquirer has not moved by Year 2, $12M Series A at $35M pre-money is available to bridge to the Year 3 strategic exit window. The pitch is **not** optimized for the fastest exit. It is optimized for **optionality**: continue building, raise growth capital, or accept a strategic offer.

**Founder economics regardless of timing.** Kyle, Ethan, Mark hold founder economics through their ownership of SMAI parent. RestorAI exit proceeds flow to SMAI per the parent-subsidiary structure (§7). At the planning case, SMAI's 50.8% stake post-Series-A delivers ~$385M to SMAI; at the acceleration scenario without Series A, SMAI's 73.2% stake delivers ~$126M.

**Stone family economics:**

- Acceleration scenario: combined Stone family exit proceeds ~$15.7M (Base case from model)
- Planning case post-Series-A: combined Stone family exit proceeds ~$48.2M (Base case from model)

**What drives the timing in practice:**

- Real strategic acquirer signals (banker fishing, advisor relationships, direct Blackstone-side conversation) accelerate the conversation toward the earlier exit point
- Slow strategic acquirer activity moves the timeline toward the planning case
- Y2 ARR and proof-gate achievement (Gate A, B, C) determines whether the team has earned the right to wait for the larger outcome

## 7. Commercial structure between SMAI and RestorAI

**Parent-subsidiary relationship:** RestorAI is a wholly-controlled subsidiary of SMAI at incorporation, with SMAI holding 80% pre-SAFE equity. The subsidiary structure preserves SMAI's optionality across other verticals while concentrating restoration value in a single sellable entity.

**IP ownership and licensing:**

- **SMAI owns** the platform IP across all field service verticals (the agent platform, the trust contract, the templated architecture, the surfaces, the spec library)
- **RestorAI receives** an arm's-length perpetual license from SMAI for use in the restoration vertical, scoped to Servpro franchisees and adjacent restoration operators
- SMAI retains direct-sales rights to all non-restoration verticals (HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, adjacent proposal-driven verticals)
- At a strategic exit, RestorAI is sold including the restoration license; SMAI retains platform IP for non-restoration use
- Improvements made by SMAI to the platform during the 12 months following a RestorAI sale flow to the acquirer under standard transition terms (specifics to be locked in counsel review)

**License fee structure (working frame, pending counsel review):** RestorAI pays SMAI a licensing fee structured as a percentage of RestorAI ARR. Exact percentage and payment cadence to be locked. The fee is set at arm's-length to satisfy multi-entity transfer-pricing discipline and to ensure SMAI captures restoration value through both its 80% ownership and an ongoing license stream.

**Founder economics:** Kyle, Ethan, and Mark hold founder economics through their ownership of ServiceMark AI, not through direct RestorAI equity grants. This preserves the multi-vertical thesis: a RestorAI exit returns capital to founders via SMAI without ending the SMAI platform. SMAI's cap table is governed separately (currently founder-owned; subject to revision if and when SMAI raises its own institutional capital).

**Governance:** SMAI leadership (Kyle, Ethan, Mark) retains operational and product decision authority for both SMAI and RestorAI. Stone family roles do not include governance authority over SMAI product decisions, platform decisions, or the trust contract. SAFE investors receive standard minority-equity protections (information rights, anti-dilution where appropriate, no board seats at the SAFE level pre-Series-A).

## 9. Pricing and offer structure

**Customer-facing pricing (three ratcheted tiers, per CP-01 §7):**

- Tier 1: Revenue Recovery — $2,000/mo/location (4 roles)
- Tier 2: Front Office — $3,825/mo/location (8 roles)
- Tier 3: Full Back Office — $6,950/mo/location (14 roles)

**Field tool add-ons (orthogonal to tier when each ships):**

- Voice Job Intake/Scoping: $800/mo/location (Q4 2026 ship target)
- Estimate Optimization Engine: $1,000/mo/location (Q1 2027 ship target, proof-gated)

**The $787K headline.** The deck anchor: a fully-loaded human equivalent across all 14 roles at fair-market salary costs $787,050 per location per year. RestorAI Tier 3 (Full Back Office) is $83,400 per location per year. Implied savings per location per year: $703,650. This is the Tier 3 ROI story.

**Founding Operator pricing:** 35% off standard tier pricing, lifetime-locked. A Tier 1 Founding Operator pays $1,300/mo/location for life. A Tier 3 Founding Operator pays $4,518/mo/location for life. The discount applies to whichever tier the operator purchases at any point.

**Bridge offering during build-out (Buc-ee's window only):** Conversion Coordinator standalone at $500/mo/location until the Sales & Revenue Recovery Team is fully shipped (M4 in the model). After M4, the standalone offering retires; new customers buy Tier 1. Existing customers on the bridge offering convert per terms set at conversion time. USDS (Jeff's group) is the reference customer for the bridge offering at this price point.

**Internal rate card vs external tiers.** Internally, every role has a published rate (governed by RestorAI Pro Forma Roles & Bundles tab; matches deck Slide 11). Internally, the rate card is the source of truth. Externally, the customer sees three tiers — not a per-role menu. À la carte single-role purchase is not a standard SKU. The rate card serves transfer pricing, financial modeling, and internal operations; the tiers serve sales discipline and packaging clarity.

**Pricing relationship to CC-04.** CC-04 v2 will lock the full pricing card. Until CC-04 v2 ratifies, the numbers in this section are working reference points anchored to the v2.10 pro forma.

## 10. Sales motion and channel discipline

Founder-led sales (Kyle, Ethan) supports Bryan's pipeline for Class A and high-priority Class B prospects. The July Servpro convention is a near-term sales-acceleration event for the Founding Operator round.

- No commitments to bespoke workflows or custom integrations
- No commitments to outcome guarantees tied to bookings or close rates
- Pilot terms enforced (Conversion Coordinator at $500/mo/location during Buc-ee's window)
- No discounts below Founding Operator pricing for non-Founding-Operator customers; no exceptions
- No exclusivity offers to franchise-investor partners or to Stone family network contacts

**Proof gates apply:**

- Gate A (Trust Boundary Proven) must be met before RestorAI scales beyond the first cohort
- Gate B (Value Proven) must be met before Stone family network introductions activate broader Servpro outreach
- Gate C (Repeatability Proven) must be met before any consideration of Servpro corporate or Blackstone outreach

## 13. Risks and open questions

**Risks:**

- **Blackstone acquires Servpro before RestorAI achieves exit-readiness.** Possible response: position RestorAI as the entity any new Servpro owner would buy on day one to retain franchisee value.
- **Servpro corporate competes by building or licensing internally.** Possible response: deep franchisee penetration as the moat; document franchisee endorsements as defensive positioning.
- **Franchise-investor concentration creates governance pressure that violates the trust contract.** Mitigation: SAFE structure does not include board seats; minority-equity protections do not extend to product or trust contract decisions; operating agreement explicit on this boundary.
- **The cumulative team-purchase rule is rejected by Founding Operators.** Per CP-01 §12, held as open tension. Some operators may want only Tier 3 functions (Operations & Back Office) without Tier 1 and Tier 2.
- **Same-product, different-SOPs frame breaks at scale.** If Servpro tenants and non-Servpro restoration tenants require materially different agent behavior beyond SOP-level configuration, RestorAI becomes a product fork.
- **Bryan Stone full-time-vs-augmenting-pay decision unresolved.** The 2026-04-23 transcript surfaced Bryan's likely hesitation about equity-only compensation. Needs explicit conversation before the April 30 lunch.
- **Founder bandwidth.** Kyle, Ethan, Mark are all part-time on RestorAI/SMAI; founder salary funding from the Founding Operator round supports half-time-plus, not full-time. If full-time is required to hit Path A acquisition timing, the round needs to close faster or be larger.
