## Purpose

This memo defines SMAI's market in a way that is defensible to investors and operators. v1.1 narrows the near-term denominator to restoration only for the next 6 to 12 months, while preserving the audited 5-vertical spine SAM as the long-arc denominator. The two-denominator structure prevents present-tense overclaim during the restoration-first window without abandoning the broader platform thesis.

The change in v1.1 is structural, not analytical. The CBP, NES, and QCEW data work in v1.0 stands. What changes is which slice of that work governs near-term GTM (restoration only) and which slice governs long-arc strategic narrative (full 5-vertical spine plus adjacent rings).

---

## What SMAI is (market definition)

SMAI builds and leases AI agents to field service operators, starting with restoration. The first agent in market — the Conversion Coordinator — runs proposal-to-response follow-through against operator-approved campaign templates. Additional agents extend the same job-centered, approval-first, event-driven discipline into adjacent moments. SMAI is positioned as the AI staffing company for restoration; the company refuses to be CRM, marketing automation, or a campaign builder.

CC-01 Platform Spine v1.5 governs product doctrine. CC-08 RestorAI Brief governs the Servpro channel commercial structure that is the primary near-term GTM motion.

## Two-denominator structure (new in v1.1)

### Near-term denominator: Restoration only

For the next 6 to 12 months, GTM scope is restoration only. The near-term denominator is:

- **Servpro franchise locations (U.S.):** ~1,800-2,000 locations across roughly 1,800 franchisees (Servpro Industries publishes 1,950+ franchise locations as the network footprint)
- **Non-Servpro restoration operators:** Independent restoration companies plus operators in restoration franchise networks other than Servpro (Belfor, Cotton, ServiceMaster Restore, PuroClean, etc.)

**Restoration employer establishment count:** From CBP 2023 and the v1.0 work, restoration employer establishments are 11,840 / 17,072 / 24,048 (Low / Base / High). Roughly 1,800-2,000 of those are Servpro franchise locations; the remainder are non-Servpro restoration operators (independent or other-network).

**Restoration nonemployer ring (additive, proxy-derived):** 32,971 / 42,948 / 51,181 (Low / Base / High). This ring is mostly single-shop independent operators below the wedge-ready threshold for the next 6 to 12 months; it remains in the long-arc denominator but is not part of active GTM scope for this window.

**Wedge-Ready restoration nonemployer SAM (8% / 15% / 25% wedge-ready factor):** 2,638 / 6,442 / 12,795 (Low / Base / High). This is the subset of restoration nonemployers that behave like serious operators with proposal volume and process maturity.

### Long-arc denominator: 5-vertical spine SAM

The 5-vertical spine SAM established in v1.0 is preserved as the long-arc denominator. Active GTM scope does not currently include the four non-restoration verticals (Roofing, HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical), but the audited backbone supports a multi-vertical platform thesis if and when restoration proof is repeatable and Gate C is met.

The long-arc denominator (preserved from v1.0):

- **Employer SAM Core (5 verticals):** 43,385 / 88,646 / 153,921 establishments (Low / Base / High)
- **Wedge-Ready Nonemployer SAM Ring (5 verticals):** 14,656 / 33,335 / 67,059 (Low / Base / High)
- **Total Combined SAM (Core + Ring, 5 verticals):** 58,041 / 121,981 / 220,980 (Low / Base / High)

These remain audited and defensible. The decision to narrow active GTM to restoration is a sequencing discipline, not a contraction of the long-arc opportunity.

### Why two denominators

- **Investor narrative honesty.** The deck and investor conversations need to reflect the present-tense narrowing without losing the long-arc thesis. CC-08 §6 frames RestorAI's exit window as restoration-specific; CC-03 v1.1's two-denominator structure is the corresponding investor-narrative discipline.
- **Anti-overclaim discipline.** CP-01 v1.1 §2 lists "no present-tense claim that SMAI already owns the full operating core today" as a hard negative. Two denominators enforce this at the market-sizing level.
- **Strategic optionality.** RestorAI is the entity that gets sold in a restoration-vertical strategic exit (CC-08 §6). SMAI retains the multi-vertical platform thesis and the 5-vertical SAM. The long-arc denominator preserves what SMAI continues with after a RestorAI exit.

## Segment scope

### Active near-term scope (next 6 to 12 months)

**Restoration only.** Servpro franchise channel via RestorAI is the primary motion (CC-05 v2.0 Channel A); direct non-Servpro restoration is the parallel motion (CC-05 Channel B).

### Deferred long-arc spine (preserved as denominator, not active GTM)

- Roofing
- HVAC
- Plumbing
- Electrical

These remain in the 5-vertical spine SAM as the long-arc denominator. Activation requires Gate C achievement in restoration plus a strategic decision to expand verticals (vs. deepen within restoration via additional agents).

### Adjacent segments (in TAM universe; not counted in near-term SAM/SOM until validated)

Preserved from v1.0:

- Bid-based construction contractors (residential remodelers; commercial and institutional contractors)
- Specialty project trades with proposal flow (windows, solar, insulation, garage doors, fencing, concrete, pool install, irrigation install)
- Commercial services sold by bid (janitorial and facilities services; security system installs; landscaping design-build)
- Professional services with proposal-driven closes (IT services and MSP projects; marketing and creative agencies; accounting and bookkeeping packages)
- B2B services sold via quoting workflows (equipment rental and service; logistics and freight brokerage; commercial maintenance contracts)

**Promotion rule (applies to any adjacent or deferred-spine segment):** It only enters active GTM when proposal parsing reliability, context pack sufficiency, stop-condition correctness, and tenant-level SOP configuration are proven without bespoke per-customer agent code.

## 4) What this means operationally

- **Active GTM is restoration-only for the next 6 to 12 months.** The Servpro franchise channel via RestorAI is the primary motion; direct non-Servpro restoration is the parallel motion. Restoration consolidators are prepare-now, activate-later.
- **The 5-vertical spine SAM is preserved as the long-arc denominator.** Activation requires Gate C achievement in restoration plus a strategic decision (continue depth in restoration via additional agents vs. expand verticals).
- **The nonemployer ring is bounded.** Restoration nonemployers in the wedge-ready ring are 2,638 / 6,442 / 12,795 Base case. Most of these are below current commercial fit but represent upside as the agent fleet matures and tenant onboarding compresses.
- **RestorAI exit is restoration-vertical-only.** A RestorAI strategic exit (CC-08 §6) sells the restoration license; SMAI retains the long-arc 5-vertical SAM and continues independently. The two-denominator structure of CC-03 v1.1 is the market-sizing companion to that structural commitment.

## 5) Facts vs assumptions vs inferences

### Facts (sourced, audited)

- CBP 2023 is an official Census release providing annual data for businesses with paid employees
- QCEW provides establishment and wage tabulations from unemployment insurance records and federal coverage
- NES 2023 is an official Census release covering businesses with no paid employees
- NES 2023 nonemployer counts are incorporated for HVAC and Plumbing at NAICS 23822 and for Electrical at NAICS 23821, with explicit allocation assumptions where required
- Servpro Industries publishes its franchise network footprint at approximately 1,950+ U.S. locations

### Assumptions (inputs or explicit modeling choices)

- HVAC and Plumbing splits inside NAICS 238220 for employer TAM and downstream SAM
- HVAC vs Plumbing allocation applied to NES NAICS 23822 nonemployer counts
- Electrical allocation from NES NAICS 23821 to 238210 using an explicit allocation band anchored to CBP employer mix
- Wedge-ready factors applied to the nonemployer ring by vertical
- SAM filter ranges by vertical (proposal dependence, email viability, managed posture acceptance, sales motion realism)
- Restoration's share of 5-vertical Employer SAM Core (~25-30%) reflects restoration's higher proposal-driven workflow density vs. HVAC, Plumbing, and Electrical (which have more break-fix and recurring service work that does not flow through proposals)

### Inferences (derived, definition-sensitive)

- Restoration employer TAM allocation that exceeds NAICS 562910 alone, reflecting that "restoration" activity is often classified outside a single NAICS and must be handled with ranges and confidence notes
- Roofing and Restoration nonemployer totals remain proxy-derived in this version due to NAICS granularity limits in the current NES extract

## 6) What must be validated to upgrade confidence

### Pilot telemetry that moves assumptions into facts

For each pilot location, capture:

- Proposal volume per week
- Percent of proposals with customer email present and deliverable
- Approval friction (time to approve, percent of plans approved)
- Stop-condition correctness (reply, bounce, pause) with zero tolerance for failures
- Measurable outcomes: replies captured and delivery truth events that change behavior
- Template variant resolution accuracy (which (job type, scenario) pair was resolved and which template version was active)

### Restoration-specific validation needs (new in v1.1)

- Actual conversion rate of Bryan Stone's qualified Servpro pipeline into Founding Operator commitments (Base case 35%; tracked weekly)
- Actual location count per Founding Operator (Base case 2.5; tracked at signing)
- Same-product, different-SOPs assumption holds across Servpro and non-Servpro restoration tenants (CP-01 v1.1 §12 open tension)
- Cumulative team-purchase rule rejection rate (CC-04 v2.0 §11 open question; CC-05 v2.0 §10 decision needed)

## Closing view

For the next 6 to 12 months, active GTM is restoration-only and the planning denominator is the restoration slice of the spine SAM (Restoration Employer SAM Core ~5,000-15,000 plus Restoration Wedge-Ready Nonemployer Ring 2,638-12,795). The Servpro franchise subset (~1,800-2,000 locations) is the primary commercial concentration via RestorAI.

The 5-vertical spine SAM (Employer Core 43,385-153,921 plus Wedge-Ready Nonemployer Ring 14,656-67,059) is preserved as the long-arc denominator and remains the addressable opportunity for SMAI's multi-vertical platform thesis. RestorAI's strategic exit, when it happens, sells the restoration license and the restoration-vertical entity; SMAI retains the long-arc denominator and continues independently in non-restoration verticals.

The two-denominator structure is the discipline that lets the company narrow active scope without abandoning long-arc opportunity. It is the market-sizing companion to CC-08's commercial structure.
